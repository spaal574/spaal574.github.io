Heresy-Era Legio Cybernetica Cohort

Strategy Rating of 2. All formations have an Initiative rating of 2+.

Special Rules

Legions of Steel. (army-wide rules)
Army composition. More than half of your army's formations must be picked from the ones that have Maniple in their name.
1 Arch-Magos per army. Paragon of Metal upgrades are 0-1 per full 1k pts.
If this army includes an Arch-Magos the first PoM must be part of his formation.
Scoring. Formations containing only units from the Robots section and/or bombots may contest but not score objectives
unless they also contain Tech-priests units/characters and/or Tech-Thralls units.
Tie-break. During a tie-break you lose 100 victory points if there are no friendly Technicians units(with or without a character) on the table.

Robot(x). Machine-creatures beyond anything servitors or cogitator controlled constructs are capable of, but without the dangers of revolt. Or so it's claimed.
Robots count as having the Cybernetica Cortex(x), Walker (or Skimmer in the case of stratos automata) and Automaton special rules.

A Formation counts as inside Cortex Controller range if at the start of it's activation at least one of it's
Robot units is within 45cm of at least one friendly Technicians unit (with or without a character).
The rules below take effect only when a robot formation is inside Cortex Controller range.
Enhanced Cyber-control. +1 to Initiative rolls during activations.
The rules below take effect only when a formation with units that have them is outside Cortex Controller range.
Cybernetica Cortex. May not perform March or Overwatch.
Cybernetica Cortex(Hunter-Killer). May not perform March or Sustained Fire.

Automaton. A formation does not receive a Blast marker when a unit with automaton is destroyed, this includes the extra Blast marker from the first casualty of a crossfire and for units destroyed for being out of formation after a move. Automaton units hit by a weapon with disrupt do take a Blast marker however. If a hit is inflicted on an automaton unit because it is in a broken formation which is receiving a Blast marker (see Blast Markers and Broken Formations) then it may attempt to save normally.

Paragon of Metal. Even among these technological marvels from the dark age of technology there are some machines that far surpass their peers. These near-unstoppable war engines could be blessed by the Omnissiah, products of exceptional genius, or influenced by forbidden knowledge. PoM count as having the Inspiring, Fearless, Invulnerable Save(or improves one already present by 1) and Walker (or Skimmer in the case of stratos automata) special rules, if this army includes a supreme commander the first PoM must be part of his formation.

Lance. An Armoured Vehicle unit with the Reinforced Armour special rule that is hit by a Lance weapon is not allowed to re-roll its saving throw. This special rule does not affect Infantry or Light Vehicles units.

The Liber Magra Veneficarum.

Elucidators have hunted and burned copies of this ancient grimoire on a thousand worlds. It contains forbidden human and xenos texts that defy the Imperial Truth, a malevolent work filled with incantations and processes of ritual meant to bring forth the horrors of the Immaterium.
The original tome, believed to date back to the Old Night, is lost but some ritualistic cultures have preserved hand-copied examples over millennia and the increasingly desperate and unscrupulous warriors in Horus' armies are rumored to have acquired some of them despite knowing its vile nature as something Mankind would do best to forget.

Formations with units with this special rule allow their player to summon daemons to the battlefield.

To summon daemons the Chaos player chooses a formation with this rule and declares its intention to summon before any action roll is made.
The player can, before rolling, sacrifice one of it's Tech-thrall units in the formation to gain 3 extra summoning points, he then rolls 2D3 to generate the number of summoning points available for this formation.
He may then summon daemons up to the number of summoning points generated and any points not used are lost.
Daemon Summoning Cost
Daemonic Beasts 2
Bloodletters 2
Plaguebearers 2
Daemonettes 2
Flamers 2


Summoned units must be set up within 5cm of a non-summoned unit in the summoning formation. They cannot be placed in enemy zones of control or impassable terrain.

Summoned units become part of the summoning formation for all rules purposes as long as they remain on the battlefield. Summoned units do not cause blast markers when they are destroyed.
Daemonic units killed in an assault count towards the number of units killed when calculating the modifiers for assault resolution. Summoned units that are destroyed are removed from play.

Summoned units remain in play until the end phase of the turn in which they were summoned. After all formations have attempted to rally any summoned units are removed from play.
This may cause the formation to break as it may now have equal or more blast markers than its number of units. When a formation breaks all of its summoned units are destroyed.



Daemonic Rage. Formations containing units with this rule receive a +1 modifier to their action test roll when attempting an engage action.

The Liber Magra Veneficarum.

Elucidators have hunted and burned copies of this ancient grimoire on a thousand worlds. It contains forbidden human and xenos texts that defy the Imperial Truth, a malevolent work filled with incantations and processes of ritual meant to bring forth the horrors of the Immaterium.
The original tome, believed to date back to the Old Night, is lost but some ritualistic cultures have preserved hand-copied examples over millennia and the increasingly desperate and unscrupulous warriors in Horus' armies are rumored to have acquired some of them despite knowing its vile nature as something Mankind would do best to forget.

Formations with units with this special rule allow their player to summon daemons to the battlefield.

To summon daemons the Chaos player chooses a formation with this rule and declares its intention to summon before any action roll is made.
The player can, before rolling, sacrifice one of it's Tech-thrall units in the formation to gain 3 extra summoning points, he then rolls 2D3 to generate the number of summoning points available for this formation.
He may then summon daemons up to the number of summoning points generated and any points not used are lost.

Daemonic Beasts 2

Summoned units must be set up within 5cm of a non-summoned unit in the summoning formation. They cannot be placed in enemy zones of control or impassable terrain.

Summoned units become part of the summoning formation for all rules purposes as long as they remain on the battlefield. Summoned units do not cause blast markers when they are destroyed.
Daemonic units killed in an assault count towards the number of units killed when calculating the modifiers for assault resolution. Summoned units that are destroyed are removed from play.

Summoned units remain in play until the end phase of the turn in which they were summoned. After all formations have attempted to rally any summoned units are removed from play.
This may cause the formation to break as it may now have equal or more blast markers than its number of units. When a formation breaks all of its summoned units are destroyed.



Daemonic Rage. Formations containing units with this rule receive a +1 modifier to their action test roll when attempting an engage action.
